The big idea

You edit the story on the story

BranchWorld already has a hardened engine and a visual map of any story. The Story Editor turns that map into a workshop: click a scene on the graph to edit it — its text, its choices, the conditions and effects — right where you can see how it connects to everything else. The global pieces (variables, timed events, endings, the deadline) live in a settings drawer. A built-in checker (the "linter") watches as you type and draws any problems straight onto the map, so you always know exactly what's broken and where — without it ever stopping you mid-thought.

The core loop

Editing a scene, step by step

  1. Click the scene on the map. It highlights, and its details open in the panel on the right — you're always editing in context, seeing how this beat connects to the rest.
  2. Write the scene. Type the title and the prose. Need to mention the time? Drop in the {{time}} token and the engine fills in the real clock — so the story can never lie about the time.
  3. Wire the choices. For each choice, pick where it leads from a menu of your scenes, and (optionally) add a condition ("only if trust ≥ 2") or an effect ("trust +1", "advance 10 minutes") — again, all from menus.
  4. Watch the checker. The map and the top bar update instantly. A red ring means a real problem (an unreachable scene, a missing ending); fix it when you're ready. Nothing is ever lost — your draft auto-saves as you go.
One story, three faces

Play · Graph · Edit

The same story powers all three views, because they all run on the one shared engine — what you edit is exactly what you play.

▶Play

Step through the story as a player would — the clock ticking, choices appearing and disappearing, the ending earned from everything you did.

▦Graph

See the whole story as a map, with the checker's findings drawn right on it. Great for understanding structure at a glance.

✎Edit

The same map, now editable. Click to change scenes, add choices, and tune the globals — with the checker guiding you live.

Saving & sharing

Drafts that look after themselves

💾Auto-saving drafts

Your work saves continuously as you type, right in the browser, and your drafts show up in the story menu next to the built-in examples — ready to Play, Graph, or keep editing.

↔New · Duplicate · Import · Export

Start fresh, copy an example like The Prater Line to take it apart, bring in a story file someone sent you, or export yours to share. The built-in examples stay read-only — duplicate one to make it yours.

A safety net, not a straitjacket

The checker informs — it never blocks

While you're building, you're constantly half-finished — a new scene with no exit yet, a choice that doesn't lead anywhere yet. So the editor never stops you mid-edit. It shows every problem live, on the map and in the top bar, and lets you save and export in any state. The hard line — "this story is too broken to ship" — is only drawn when it actually matters, the same way a spell-checker underlines a typo without locking your keyboard.
